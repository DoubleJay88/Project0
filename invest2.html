Basic Ingredients & Methods

The starting point of any stock investing has to be from its basics. We will touch on just the essential points without dwelling deep into it as these information are readily available in greater detail all over the internet, libraries and book stores.

Generally, we can either trade a stock or invest in it. When trading, our primary objective is to buy and sell a stock for short term profits. The short term fluctuation in prices is of concern to us. In contrast, investing in a stock is to buy a stock and to hold it for long term profits and dividends payout. The end goal of both trading and investing are similar in that they both pursue profits but along different path and time frame. For the purpose of this site, we will refer to investing to mean either trading or investing.

Now that one has decided to invest in stocks, perhaps the first question on ones mind would be "Where do I start?" and "Which stocks should I buy?". Well, first, we need to understand the methods of analyzing stocks of which there are two (2) methods, i.e. Fundamental analysis (FA) and Technical analysis (TA). Nested within these 2 methods is Quantitative analysis (QA) where investors calculates the company's financial ratios such as earnings per share or the more complex discounted cash flow calculations.

FA is the method of anlysing stocks by evaluating the company's worth by reviewing it's financial health via financial statements, earnings trend and other financial information. Unlike FA, TA evaluates stocks by studying price trends and chart patterns on the assumption that past trading patterns and price movements give an insight into the future movement of the stock.
