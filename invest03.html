Reality

Investment is an Artform

Acquiring a working knowledge of FA and TA, does not mean that one is fully equipped to invest successfully but it does gives a headstart. In reality, investing is an art form developed over a long period of time interspersed with success and failures, losses and profits.

FA gives us an insight into certain sectors and companies and helps us to identify and target the stock that is worth investing in. TA helps us to time our entry and exit of a stock. This FA/TA combination attempts to minimize our investing risk while maximizing our return. Any attempt to discard this approach is akin to gambling where one might be better off going to the casino instead, which commands a similar risk/benefit ratio of 50:50.

In reality, there are no holy grail of investing techniques . There are some who rely heavily on a strict FA approach. Some are solely into TA, the pure technicians. Then there are those who employ a combination of FA and TA. Yet there are some who are neither FA nor TA, relying instead on experience, hunch and price-volume movements and some solely on stock tips. And lastly, there is also an emerging group of stock investors who utilizes softwares to trade, an extension of the algorithm trading system employed by big, expert investors.

Whichever technique one choses is fine as long as it suits one's style and yields positive results, measured of course by profits. There is really no right or wrong in stock investing but the one crucial factor that needs to be managed is emotion which can ultimately define failure or success. As humans, emotions is the most difficult to control. Only when we are able to discard emotions during investing can one be consistently successful in stock investing.

  • Point & Figure (P&F) Charts
